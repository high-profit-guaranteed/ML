A consortium of Christian health-care cost-sharing ministries sued Colorado, claiming the state’s new law requiring the ministries to disclose their financial data and organizational structure violated their First Amendment rights.

The Alliance of Health Care Sharing Ministries filed a complaint Thursday in the US District Court for the District of Colorado against the state’s insurance commissioner, alleging the state’s requirement that sharing ministries report financial data, membership information, affiliated providers, communications materials, and other operational details violated “the Free Exercise rights of ministries to be free from laws that target religious exercise.”
